🎯 CRO Priority
Mobile converts at less than half the rate of desktop
Mobile accounts for 68% of organic sessions but generates a key event rate of just 0.55% — compared to 1.16% on desktop. That's a 2.1× gap. Given the session volume (419k mobile vs 180k desktop), closing even half of this gap would nearly double total key events. A focused mobile UX audit of the top 5 landing pages is likely the highest-leverage action in the next 90 days.
Mobile: 2,311 key events from 419k sessions · Desktop: 2,089 from 180k
⚠ Risk Signal
Homepage dependence is an organic channel risk
The homepage (/) generated 147,716 organic sessions — 24% of all organic traffic. That's more than the next 10 landing pages combined. This level of concentration means a single SERP change or algorithm adjustment could materially impact the entire organic channel. Diversifying organic traffic across more category, guide, and model pages should be a strategic priority.
Homepage: 147,716 sessions · #2 page: /car-leasing/deals at 23,484
📝 Content Strategy
Blog traffic doesn't convert — a linking strategy could change that
Blog content like "Six new driving laws in 2026" and "Best Top Gear episodes" drives significant traffic (5,124 and 3,496 sessions respectively) but generates zero key events. These are informational readers, not buyers — but they're already on the site. A structured internal linking approach that routes editorial traffic toward relevant deal pages could improve the commercial value of this content without creating anything new.
Top 7 blog pages: 17,938 sessions · 0 key events combined
🔍 Anomaly
Defender Commercial is an outlier van page worth a CRO test
The /van-leasing/land-rover/defender-commercial page drove 10,577 sessions — more than 3× any other single van model page. This is almost certainly driven by search volume around the Defender Commercial's 2025 tax treatment changes. With an 87% engagement rate, the content is relevant. This page is likely leaving conversions on the table and warrants a dedicated CTA or landing page optimisation test.
/van-leasing/land-rover/defender-commercial: 10,577 sessions · 87.1% eng rate · 113 key events
📅 Seasonal Pattern
January is the strongest organic month — plan for it
The data shows a clear seasonal uplift in early January — sessions peaked at 8,051 on 13 Jan, and the weeks of 6 Jan and 13 Jan were the highest-traffic weeks in the 90-day window. This aligns with typical new year car research behaviour. Planning content refreshes, promotional pushes, and paid support to coincide with this annual window (late Dec through Jan) could amplify already-strong organic intent.
Peak week (6–12 Jan): 51,115 sessions · vs 90-day weekly avg ~47,400
🔧 Tracking Gap
Named conversion events are not appearing in GA4
The conversion events specified in the Phase 1 brief — complete_finance_form, lead_order_online, lead_callback_request, lead_enquiry, lead_newsletter_signup — do not appear in the current GA4 event data. The property shows 4,486 key events across 90 days but these can't be broken down by conversion type. This should be investigated and resolved before the dashboard is put in front of the NVC team.
